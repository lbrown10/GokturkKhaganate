Political Structure

The Tribal Structure
Tribes were usually ruled by the male elders called Aqsaqal (literally, the white-bearded ones). Tribes were usually just very large extended families, and so decisions often came down to a council or single leader of these elders. Most nomadic tribes had variations between them as to how power was distributed.

Often one tribe would take power over several, and an individual who held power over multiple tribes was called a Khan, or Khagan (alternatively spelled Qaghan). This term first came into use with the G√∂kt√ºrk Khaganate, although it is now generally applied to all of the Steppe Nomad states that followed a similar political structure. Although the Khagan would nominally have total authority, in practice the nature of the nomadic tribes meant that each tribe usually maintained a relatively high degree of autonomy. Rebellions were frequent as tribes would have varying opinions on the succession of Khagans, and would often try and reject the authority of the Ashina clan entirely.

While previously leaders of nomadic confederations had held roughly equal power, especially in the Kurultai, which was a gathering for what was more or less an election of the new ruler after a previous Khagan died. The invention of the G√∂kt√ºrk Khaganate was to make a more stratified system, where a small number of tribes ruled various regions of smaller weaker tribes. This allowed the Khagan to have control over a few lesser Khans, rather than needing to directly enforce unity between large numbers of tribes.

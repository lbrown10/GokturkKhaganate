Tengrism

The major religious beliefs of the Turks and other Tribes was a form of shamanism referred to as Tengism. It followed a system of beliefs centering around the all-powerful sky god Tengri. The belief system was non-dogmatic and not based around any known central texts, but seemed to encompass a wide variety of cultural practices. Tengrism was also widely accepting of other religions as a result.

It is an animistic religion focused on nature, emphasizing a wide number of spirits that inhabited all things. Natural features such as rivers and mountains could also have great spiritual importance.
Buddhism
Buddhism had limited spread in the Turkic Khaganate. As the Turks spread south into Sassanid territory, especially regions such as Sogdia, they encountered a small number of Buddhists. The silk road would assist in transporting these beliefs further, although it was always a minority religion.

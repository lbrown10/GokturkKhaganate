An Empire of the Horse

Central Asia from 552-744

From the 6th to 8th centuries, central asia was domintated by the Turks, a nomadic tribe that united huge territories and diverse cultures. For 200 years, the Ashina clan would bring a unity to central asia that would not return until the Mongol invasions. Crossing path with the Byzantine, Sassanid, Chinese, and Arab Empires, these nomads would shape the future of Asia and beyond for ages to come.

The empire was founded from a unification of independent tribes, then split into two parts after a civil war, before both East and West were eventually absorbed into the Tang Empire. Soon a revolution restored the Turks to control, but this empire would eventually fall to internal rebellions and be replaced by rule from other tribes.




Ancient History

Scythians
11th century BCE - 2nd century CE

Scythia actually represents a series of kingdoms that controlled the lands to the north and east of the Black Sea. They were characterized by the use of horses, which were important in controlling and living off of the vast land area of the Central Asian Steppe.

The First Khaganate
(552-581)

Out of the Nomadic Tribes that covered all of Central Asia, the Ashina clan rose up to unite many of them into a single empire.

Civil War
(581-603)

A succession crisis led to a civil war that resulted in the fracturing of the empire into two independent entities.

The Eastern Empire
(581-630)

The Eastern Turkic Khaganate was dominated by its interactions with the Tang Empire in China, which after decades of raiding and war eventually took control of the Turks.

The Western Empire
(581-657)

The Western Turkic Khaganate managed the northern silk road and fought against the Byzantine and Sassanid empires.

The Second Khaganate
(682-744)

After being ruled by the Tang dynasty, a revolution restored control of the Empire to Turkic control.

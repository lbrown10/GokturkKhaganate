The Second Turkic Khaganate

(682-744)

Rise
The Turks had never settled easily to Chinese rule and there were a number of attempts at rebellion. One such person, Ilterish, was chieftan of the Ashina tribe and led a rebellion against the Tang in 681. Within a few years he had united many of the Turkish tribes and had restored the boundaries of much of the Eastern Empire. He also began to launch raids into the Tang Empire.

His brother Qapaghan succeeded him in 692, and led the new Turkic Khagante until 716. He was able to further expand and united the tribes, eventually leading them into battle against a Tang Empire troubled by civil war. He was able to eventually force tributes to be paid to stem the raiding. Qapaghan also expanded the empire further to the west, recovering much of the old lands of the Turkic Khaganate until he suddenly encountered the Arabs at Samarkand in 712.
Fall
The next few decades saw several Khagans come and go as infighting and assassination hampered any sense of stable rule. This gradually led to less centralized control, as various family members of the Ashina tribe took control of various parts of the empire. In 741 four khagans claimed the title of ruler of the Turkic Khaganate. In 742, A new coalition of tribes, led by the Uyghur tribe, rebelled to take control of the Khaganate.
